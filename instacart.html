Instacart Customer Profiles

Context

Instacart is an online grocery store that operates through an app. Stakeholders want to target different customers with applicable marketing campaigns to see whether they influence the sale of their products.

Objective

Perform an initial data and exploratory analysis to derive insights and suggest strategies for better segmentation based on the provided criteria.

Deliverable(s)

A final report in Excel containing data, analysis, and insights. Includes tables and visualizations to effectively communicate recommendations on a marketing strategy based on customer purchasing habits.

Tools Used

Microsoft Excel for reporting, with Jupyter Python and relevant libraries (pandas, NumPy, matplotlib, and seaborn) for analysis and visualizations.

The Data

“The Instacart Online Grocery Shopping Dataset 2017”, Accessed from www.instacart.com/datasets/grocery-shopping-2017 via Kaggle on 10/10/2023.

Used these datasets: customers, orders, products, departments, and orders_products_prior. Collectively, over 30 million entries.

First step - Cleaning Data

The data was checked for cleanliness and consistency (missing values, duplicates, and format changes). Cleaned and checked consistency of the customers, orders, and orders_products_prior data sets. The departments data set was reformatted into a data dictionary data frame to assist in analysis.

Second step - Merging Data

Data was merged into a single dataset with over 30 million entries and over 30 columns, using primary keys of order_id or user_id depending on the dataset.

Third Step - Segmenting Customers

To get to the heart of what the Analysis is about, the customer profiles, we need to segment the customers into groups. Using the loc() function, it is very simple to slice into data frames and segment groups.

Example of code to assist in creating a customer profile, specifically a loyalty flag on a customer based on max_order number.

# Applying loyalty flag to max_order column using loc function
ords_prods_merge.loc[ords_prods_merge['max_order'] > 40, 'loyalty_flag'] = 'Loyal customer'
ords_prods_merge.loc[(ords_prods_merge['max_order'] <= 40) & (ords_prods_merge['max_order'] > 10), 'loyalty_flag'] = 'Regular customer'
ords_prods_merge.loc[ords_prods_merge['max_order'] <= 10, 'loyalty_flag'] = 'New customer'

Fourth step - Insights and Visualizing

The busiest time of day is midday, from 9 am to 5 pm. 84% of all orders happen within this timeframe. Every hour in this timeframe has more than 1.5 million orders.

The most popular products ordered by customers are' produce' and 'dairy eggs' products. These two departments make up 52%, over half of all orders.

Regular and New Customers make up 90% of the customer base.

Loyal Customers only make up 10% of all customers, yet they are 33% of all orders. Regular Customers order at 51% but are 47% of the customer base. New Customers are 42% of the customer base but are only 16% of all orders.

Majority of customers are “Mature Family”, meaning they are over 30 years old, married, and have dependants. 86% of all customers are Mature Family or Childless single. Analysis showed that ordering habits did not change between these customer profiles.

Recommendations

  • Run ads early in the morning, 6 - 8 am, and later afternoon, 6 - 8 pm. These are times are not as popular to purchase, but still have enough traffic to promote good growth and have potential for revenue gain.
  • Run ads to promote 'Snacks' and 'Beverages' by at least 5%. These two departments have a lot of potential for higher ordering frequency.
  • Target ads on Regular Customers. Being the largest customer population, and who order most, they have the most profit potential. Shifting them into Loyal Customers if they order enough is the goal at 10% increase.
  • Run ads or promotions for New Customers, with a goal to increase their purchasing. If they can be shifted into Regular Customers, the goal is to increase Regular Customer by 5%.
  • Run ads to increase number of Mature Family customers by 10%. With that base increasing, it can only mean more profit.
  • Run ads to increase number of Childless Single customers by 5% for the same reasoning as to do it for Mature Family customers.
