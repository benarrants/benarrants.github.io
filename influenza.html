Staffing for Influenza Season

Context

A medical staffing agency provides temporary workers to clinics and hospitals on an as-needed basis. During flu season, vulnerable populations (individuals age 65+) are particular susceptible.

Objective

Present insights to stakeholders to determine when to send staff, and how many to each state for the flu season of 2018.

Deliverable(s)

Create a Tableau Storyboard to tell the compelling tale of the data.

Tools Used

Microsoft Excel, and Tableau Public for Visualizations.

The Data

  • Influenza deaths by geography, time, age, and gender, 2009-2017. Source: CDC Data Set
  • Population data by geography 2009-2017. Source: US Census Bureau Data Set
  • Tableau Storyboard

Initial Analysis Steps

  1. Create data profiles, detailing bias and source quality or integrity.
  2. Data Integration and Transformation - Merged the two datasets on a primary key within Excel.
  3. Calculate descriptive statistics of variables, including standard deviation and variance.
  4. Formulate a Hypothesis test to compare if there is a significant difference among variables.
  5. Test for correlation between variables- Tested the correlation between death count and population over 65+.

Step 6 - Insights and Data Visualization

This map shows where high death count is concentrated and which states are affected by a high percentage of death. Still, this isn't the full story.

In the chart below, states are sorted by population categorized by High, Medium, and Low Need. 810,000 is the mean age of the 65+ population. All states below that are labeled as Low-Need. 1,700,000 is one standard deviation above the mean and everything below that and to the mean is Medium-Need. Everything above the deviation is High-Need. In addition to population, the state's influenza deaths are also shown. This chart can clearly show the effect of death count vs death rate. New York is a perfect example.

Recommendations

  • California, New York, Florida, Pennsylvania, Texas, Illinois, and Ohio have the most need
  • According to previous CDC data, Influenza season varies but usually starts around week 40, increasing in severity until reaching a peak and decreasing back to around week 20.
  • The data for staffing numbers is unavailable currently, but following projected forecasting, California and Pennsylvania will each have about 5% fewer deaths due to Influenza in 2018. Because of this, 9% of those staffing resources can move to Texas, New York, and Ohio where they have, collectively, a 9% increase in deaths forecasted.
  • How many staff members to send can be better answered when the data is available.
  • To monitor the effectiveness of the staffing plan, there should be an active measure of the death rate and death count for each state.
  • If a state has an abnormally high metric, then it likely needs for staff.
